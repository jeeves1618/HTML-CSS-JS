The Basics

  • First Things...

    Your client doesn't know what he wants. They know their business, but they do not know how to express their business through a website or mbile. Encourage your client not to cram information on the site. Find a way for the client to INVEST in the project. Have a single person responsible for decisions. Limit the number of revisions (or free revisions, if it is a paying job) upfront.

  • Get Help!

    Involve others when and if they are needed. You may not know all the technologies in the world. By involving others, you learn from them as well. Get an idea of what the client has right now. Talk to the existing users and understand how useful it is. Take help from the internet. Explore the examples of relevant sites to help the client figure out what they really want.

  • Take a field trip

    Yes... thats right. Understand the operations at a very granular level. Find out what the client wants to improve in their operations. Show them the different websites and ask what they like most in those websites. It could be color, fonts or anything. Ask the client who the single point of contact is. Get the commitment that the client will provide all required information.

  • Design Overview

    Don't start coding. Do your wireframes through websites like https://balsamiq.com/. Once the wireframes are ready, get the colors added either through phtoshop or powerpoint. Get the combinations from https://coolors.co/palettes, if colors are not your forte. These wireframes and color combinations don't have to be accurate. But, it should pivot you in the right direction.

  • Learn the Building Blocks

    To be precise, learn the underlying tech first. They are the constants in the endless fluxes of frameworks thrown at us. The basic building blocks of web development twenty years back were HTML, CSS and JavaScript. And they are still the foundation over which bootstraps, ReachJS etc. are built. So, gain expertise in these three before flaunting your skills in the frameworks.

  • Set up the environment

    You need a code editor. My choice is Visual Studio Code. You can use Atoms, notepadd or whatever editor you like. Please ensure you are connected to the internet since we will CDN the content necessary for Bootstrap. Have a github account. It will help you to host the site in github pages. Chrome, because of its sophisticated developer tools, is my browser option.
